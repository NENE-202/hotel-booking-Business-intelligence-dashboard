# Hotel Booking Business Intelligence Dashboard

## Project Background

This project analyzes hotel booking data to uncover insights into revenue performance, customer behavior, booking cancellations, and seasonal demand patterns.

The hospitality industry relies heavily on data-driven decision-making to maximize occupancy, increase revenue, reduce cancellations, and improve guest satisfaction. This analysis focuses on identifying operational opportunities that can improve profitability and customer experience across hotel operations.

The dashboard explores four key business areas:

* Executive Business Performance
* Cancellation Analysis
* Seasonal Demand Analysis
* Guest Experience Analysis

Insights and recommendations are provided to support hotel management in improving revenue generation, customer retention, and operational efficiency.

---

## Business Questions

This project seeks to answer the following questions:

1. How is the hotel performing financially?
2. Which customer segments generate the most revenue?
3. What factors contribute to booking cancellations?
4. Which periods generate the highest revenue and ADR?
5. How do guest behaviors influence hotel performance?
6. Which markets and customer groups should management prioritize?

---

# Executive Summary

## Overview of Findings

The analysis revealed four major business insights:

### Revenue Concentration

Transient-Party customers generated the highest revenue contribution, followed by Transient customers.

### High Cancellation Rate

The hotel experiences a cancellation rate of approximately 37%, representing a significant revenue leakage opportunity.

### Strong Seasonality

Revenue and ADR fluctuate considerably across arrival months, indicating seasonal demand patterns.

### Guest Value Increases with Special Requests

Guests making multiple special requests consistently show higher ADR values, suggesting a relationship between guest engagement and spending behavior.

# Dashboard Overview

## Page 1: Executive Report

This dashboard provides an overview of business performance using:

* Total Revenue
* Total Guests
* Average Daily Rate
* Cancellation Rate
* Revenue by Customer Type
* Revenue by Hotel Type
* Monthly Revenue Trend

### Key Findings

* Total Revenue exceeded $828K.
* City Hotels generated significantly more revenue than Resort Hotels.
* Transient-Party customers represented the largest revenue source.

---

## Page 2: Cancellation Analysis

This dashboard investigates booking cancellation behavior.

### Metrics Analyzed

* Cancellation Rate by Market Segment
* Cancellation Rate by Hotel Type
* Cancellation Rate by Deposit Type

### Key Findings

* Undefined and Group market segments showed the highest cancellation rates.
* Non-Refundable deposits experienced the lowest cancellation likelihood.
* City Hotels experienced higher cancellation volumes than Resort Hotels.

### Recommendation

Implement stronger deposit policies and targeted retention strategies for high-risk customer segments.

---

## Page 3: Seasonal Analysis

This dashboard examines demand patterns across arrival months.

### Metrics Analyzed

* Average ADR by Month
* Revenue by Month
* Revenue by Length of Stay

### Key Findings

* October generated the highest monthly revenue.
* August recorded the highest ADR.
* Short-stay bookings contributed the majority of revenue.

### Recommendation

Increase marketing activities before peak demand periods and optimize pricing using seasonal demand forecasts.

---

## Page 4: Guest Experience Report

This dashboard focuses on guest behavior and customer value.

### Metrics Analyzed

* Revenue by Customer Type
* ADR by Number of Special Requests
* Revenue by Country

### Key Findings

* Transient-Party customers generated over $500K in revenue.
* ADR increased as the number of special requests increased.
* Revenue was generated from a diverse international customer base.

### Recommendation

Develop premium guest packages targeting customers who demonstrate higher engagement through special requests.

---

# Key Insights

## Revenue Optimization

Transient-Party and Transient customers should remain primary marketing targets due to their strong revenue contribution.

## Cancellation Reduction

A 37% cancellation rate presents significant revenue recovery opportunities through improved booking policies and customer retention initiatives.

## Seasonal Pricing

Revenue and ADR patterns support implementing dynamic pricing strategies during high-demand periods.

## Customer Experience

Guests making more special requests typically pay higher ADR, suggesting opportunities for personalized upselling and premium service offerings.

---

# Recommendations

### Reduce Booking Cancellations

* Encourage Non-Refundable deposit options.
* Offer incentives for confirmed reservations.
* Develop cancellation prediction models.

### Improve Revenue Management

* Implement dynamic pricing during peak months.
* Focus marketing campaigns on high-value customer segments.

### Enhance Guest Experience

* Personalize services for guests with multiple special requests.
* Create premium packages targeting higher-value customers.

### Seasonal Planning

* Increase staffing and room availability during peak periods.
* Launch promotional campaigns during low-demand months.

---

# Assumptions & Limitations

Several assumptions were made during the analysis:

* Dataset accurately reflects booking activity.
* Revenue calculations are complete and accurate.
* Customer segment classifications are correctly assigned.
* Seasonal patterns are representative of broader hotel demand trends.

Limitations:

* Analysis is based on historical booking data.
* External factors such as economic conditions, tourism trends, and local events were not included.
* Customer satisfaction survey data was unavailable.
